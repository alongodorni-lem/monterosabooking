The Macugnaga Booking project

A digital system to book local experiences: a coordinated catalogue, online booking and widgets to share with hotels and portals.

About us

One destination, one voice

Macugnaga Booking is the online booking portal for authentic experiences at the foot of Monte Rosa: hikes, wellness, Walser culture, outdoor and ideas for famiglie e weekend — within reach of Milan, Lake Maggiore and the cities of the plain.

The project is conceived and developed by Lem s.r.l. per Unione Montana Valli dell’Ossola — Piedmont Region, within FOSMIT · Fondo Sviluppo Montagne Italiane, together with local operators.

The goal is to overcome fragmented offer: connecting activities, facilities and local resources in a coordinated, digital and replicable model for sustainable, slow and experiential tourism.

How it started

From the territory to the portal

A path built with operators, not from a desk.

Preliminary work

Meetings with local operators to understand existing offers, audiences, quality requirements and the needs of those who run experiences on the ground.

Experiences catalogue

Building a single catalogue with complete sheets: description, images, duration, logistics, price and booking tips.

Booking engine

Activating the online booking system: date search, secure payment and instant confirmation with guides’ direct contacts.

Promotion

Coordinated communication around the destination: digital content, materials for operators and showcasing experiences on the portal.

Sharing

The booking widget, wherever you need it

Hotels, mountain huts, tourism portals and other partners can embed the same booking engine on their site without creating competing portals. The catalogue stays updated in one place; booking flows can be tracked by referral channel.

If you want to embed the booking engine on your site, write to macugnagabooking@gmail.com: we will tell you how to proceed.

Next steps

Future developments

The portal is designed to grow with the territory. Planned workstreams include:

  • adding new activities and offers from municipalities in the Unione Montana when they meet the required standards;
  • greater self-management for operators and local contacts;
  • collaboration with the tourist office to strengthen local information and promotion.
