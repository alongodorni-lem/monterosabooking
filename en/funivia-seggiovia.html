Cableway and chairlift in quota

Two areas

Belvedere chairlifts and Monte Moro cableways

In Macugnaga the ski lifts connect the valley floor to two distinct areas.

On the Belvedere side, chairlifts from Pecetto climb to Alpe Burki (mid station, about 1593 m) and Belvedere (about 1914 m): a natural balcony on Monte Rosa’s “Himalayan face”.

On the Monte Moro side, cableways link Staffa to Alpe Bill and, with the second section, to Passo Moro (about 2870 m), one of the best-known scenic passes in the Anzasca Valley.

Source and updates: macugnagamonterosaski.com · destinazione macugnaga-monterosa.it

At altitude

What to experience with the lifts

When sections are running, taking the chairlift or cableway opens views, alpine pastures and trails without facing the elevation gain on foot first — ideal for families, weekends and slow-pace mountain lovers.

Belvedere and Alpe Burki

Views of the east face, silence and pastures. At Alpe Burki, huts and refuges with alpine cuisine: a perfect base for a half-day at low–mid altitude.

Walks from Belvedere

From Belvedere you can continue on foot toward Rifugio Zamboni-Zappa, routes on the Belvedere glacier and Lago delle Locce — trails of varying difficulty; choose by fitness and conditions.

Alpe Bill and Passo Moro

The cableway brings you to Alpe Bill and, when the second section is open, to Passo Moro: 360° views of the Alps and toward Switzerland, with the Madonna della Neve near the top station.

Summer and winter

Mountains in every season

In summer the lifts — in operating periods published by the company — make trekking, mountain biking and panoramic days among meadows, woods and high altitude easier.

In winter Belvedere and Monte Moro are the heart of skiing and snowboarding in Macugnaga, with slopes and links described on the official map.

Individual lifts or sections may be closed for maintenance, weather or technical updates: do not rely on hearsay — check live status before planning your day.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions about cableway and chairlift

Which lifts are there in Macugnaga?

Belvedere chairlifts (Pecetto–Burki–Belvedere) and Monte Moro cableways (Staffa–Alpe Bill–Passo Moro).

What do you see and do at altitude?

Monte Rosa panoramas, stops at alpine pastures (Burki, Bill), walks toward refuges and the Belvedere glacier; at Passo Moro (when reachable via the second section) 360° views and the Madonna della Neve.

How do I know if they are open today?

Only from the official site: macugnagamonterosaski.com/impianti. Times and status of individual sections can change even in season.

Can I book lift tickets here?

No. This is the booking portal for guided experiences and cultural visits. For tickets and skipasses use the lift company’s channels.

How to combine them with a weekend?

A trip up (if lifts are running) pairs with Walser House, gold mine and outdoor activities. Ideas on Weekend and bookings on Experiences.
